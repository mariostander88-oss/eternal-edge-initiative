This document details the metallurgical principles behind the Eternal Edge Initiative, specifically focusing on the intersection of **High-Entropy Alloys (HEAs)** and **Bulk Metallic Glasses (BMGs)**.

## 1. The Core Concept: Confusion Principle
Conventional alloys (like steel) crystallize when they cool. Atoms line up in neat rows (grains).
* **The Problem:** Grain boundaries are weak points where corrosion and cracks start.
* **The Solution:** Make the atoms so different in size and chemical affinity that they get "confused" and cannot organize into crystals before they freeze.

By mixing 5+ elements (Ti, Zr, Ni, Cu, Pd), we create a "traffic jam" at the atomic level. When cooled rapidly, the atoms freeze in a chaotic, liquid-like arrangement. This is the **Amorphous State**.

## 2. High-Entropy Stabilization
In standard thermodynamics, nature prefers order (low entropy). However, Gibbs Free Energy ($\Delta G$) is defined as:

$$\Delta G = \Delta H - T \Delta S$$

Where:
* $\Delta H$ is Enthalpy
* $T$ is Temperature
* $\Delta S$ is Entropy

**Our Strategy:** By mixing 5 elements, we maximize the **Entropy of Mixing ($\Delta S_{mix}$)**.
This lowers the Gibbs Free Energy, making the chaotic solution *more stable* than the ordered crystal structure at high temperatures. This is the "High-Entropy Effect."

## 3. Elemental Roles in the Matrix

| Element | Atomic Radius (pm) | Role in Alloy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Titanium (Ti)** | 147 | **Base Matrix:** Provides high strength-to-weight ratio. |
| **Zirconium (Zr)** | 160 | **Glass Former:** Its large size disrupts the crystal lattice of Ti. |
| **Nickel (Ni)** | 124 | **Binder:** Smaller atom that fills gaps (interstitial sites), increasing density. |
| **Copper (Cu)** | 128 | **Eutectic Former:** Lowers the overall melting point, making casting easier. |
| **Palladium (Pd)** | 137 | **Noble Stabilizer:** Drastically improves corrosion resistance and glass-forming ability (GFA). |

## 4. Critical Cooling Rate ($R_c$)
To achieve the amorphous state, we must cool the alloy faster than the time it takes for crystals to nucleate. This is visualized in the **Time-Temperature-Transformation (TTT)** diagram.

* **Carbon Steel:** $R_c \approx 100 \text{ K/s}$
* **Our Target Alloy:** $R_c \approx 10^3 - 10^4 \text{ K/s}$

We achieve this using **Copper Mold Casting** with active water cooling or **Suction Casting** for smaller geometries.
